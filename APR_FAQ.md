# Presage Protocol — Interest Rate & APR FAQ

This document outlines how interest rates and Annual Percentage Rates (APR) are determined within the Presage Protocol.

### Q1: How is the APR dictated in the protocol?
The APR is not a fixed number that we set manually. Instead, it is determined dynamically by the **Interest Rate Model (IRM)** integrated from Morpho Blue, specifically the **Adaptive Curve IRM**. 

This model automatically adjusts the interest rate based on **market utilization** — the ratio of how much capital is being borrowed versus how much is supplied in a specific market.

### Q2: Do we decide that suppliers will get X% and borrowers will pay Y%?
No, we do not set fixed rates. The rates are market-driven and mathematically determined:

*   **Borrower APR:** Calculated by the IRM curve based on current utilization. If many people want to borrow (high utilization), the rate increases to attract more suppliers. If few people borrow (low utilization), the rate decreases.
*   **Supplier APR:** Suppliers receive the interest paid by borrowers, distributed proportionally.
    *   *Example:* If the borrow rate is 10% and only 50% of the supplied funds are borrowed (50% utilization), the supplier APR will be approximately 5% (10% borrow rate × 0.50 utilization).
*   **The Spread:** The "gap" between what borrowers pay and what suppliers get is determined by the utilization ratio and any protocol fees.

### Q3: Who has control over these rates? Can we change them?
The Interest Rate Model (IRM) for a specific market is selected when the market is first opened. 
*   **Immutability:** Once a market is created on Morpho Blue, its IRM is immutable (cannot be changed). This ensures transparency and prevents "rug-pulling" via interest rate manipulation.
*   **Governance-less:** The **Adaptive Curve IRM** operates autonomously based on on-chain math. It adjusts itself to find the "equilibrium" rate where supply meets demand.
*   **Creation Control:** As the protocol creators, we choose the IRM address used for all new markets we launch.

### Q4: Is there a "Spread" or "Protocol Fee"?
Morpho Blue supports a protocol fee (a percentage of the interest paid by borrowers). 
*   Currently, Presage uses the standard Morpho Blue markets. 
*   If a protocol fee is active, it creates a small spread where a portion of the interest goes to the protocol rather than the suppliers. 
*   Presage itself does not currently add an extra layer of fees on top of the Morpho interest rates.

### Q5: What happens if there are no borrowers?
If utilization is 0% (no one is borrowing), the IRM will automatically trend the borrow rate toward 0%. Since no interest is being generated by borrowers, the supplier APR will also be 0%. This creates a natural market signal: lenders will move capital to more active markets, and borrowers will be incentivized by the near-zero cost of capital.

### Q6: What makes the curve "Adaptive"?
The curve is designed to target a specific "Goldilocks" utilization rate (typically 90%).
*   **Too High (>90%):** If borrowing is too high, it becomes difficult for suppliers to withdraw. The curve "shifts up" over time to increase the cost of borrowing and attract more lenders.
*   **Too Low (<90%):** If borrowing is too low, capital is sitting idle. The curve "shifts down" to make borrowing more attractive.
This mechanism ensures there is almost always enough liquidity for suppliers to exit their positions.

### Q7: Why use this model instead of deciding the rates ourselves?
1.  **Safety:** Automated rates prevent liquidity crunches that occur when fixed rates are set too low.
2.  **Efficiency:** We don't need a manual "Risk Committee" to update rates every time the market moves.
3.  **Trust:** Users can verify the interest rate math directly on Etherscan/Morpho, making the protocol more institutional-grade and transparent.

### Q8: What happens to the APR when the outcome is resolved?
When the underlying prediction market is resolved, the loan collateral (the outcome tokens) either becomes worth $1 or $0.
*   **Post-Resolution:** The market will naturally wind down. Borrowers will either repay their loans to reclaim their $1-valued tokens or walk away if their tokens are worth $0. 
*   **Interest Stop:** Once a borrower repays, they stop accruing interest. 
*   **Supplier Impact:** As the pool shrinks post-resolution, the utilization will shift, and the IRM will adjust the rates accordingly until the market is fully settled.
